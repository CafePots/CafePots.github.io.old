What is Kali
Kali Linux, created by Offensive-security, is a popular distribution of Linux used by penetration testers and hackers alike. Kali comes with many preinstalled hacking tools such as Wireshark, hash cat, and Metasploit plus it is all free and easy to access for many different platforms. ( https://www.kali.org/docs/introduction/ )

  Hardware

While you at least need a decent computer for many hacks there are other components for different things.
Network Adapters

Alfa adapters have been the best experience for me as they have a fairly easy installation, though any network adapter that supports monitor and injection modes will work for Wi-Fi hacking.

• Tp-link adapters
• Alfa network adapters
• Off brand ones etc.
Computers, Laptops and Desktops

Any computer can work for hacking though the higher the specifications the better as some programs will use all the graphics, CPUs, and memory like hash cat.

Raspberry PI
A decent credit card sized computer that runs on Linux which can be priced from 50 to 200 dollars.

Ethernet

Any ethernet cord should do but it allows for faster connections and sometimes without any authentication, which means if there is an open port you can easily access whatever device you are trying to.

Hak 5 products

Hak 5 makes an array of products to automate Wi-Fi and computer hacking such as the wi-fi pineapple and the rubber ducky.

Hacking_History_and_What_is_IT
While many first think of “computers and large companies,” though nearly everything can be hacked. Anywhere from phones, people, and computers can be hacked by many different methods. Many of the first accounts of hacking started as pranks by teenagers in phone companies who would switch the lines on phone calls. The definition of hacking is vague itself as another use of the word “hack” was at MIT where students would create programs to automate shortcuts and complete tasks faster due to the limited access to the computers in the 1960s. Hacking is a broad term that can be used for anything to really be made easier such as “life-hacks” or have to do with a high knowledge of computers. ( http://plaza.ufl.edu/ysmgator/projects/project2/history.html )
Phreaking ( phone, free & freak )
In the 1970s, hacking became more than just a series of pranks as it started to involve avoiding phone payments. Phone phreaking was discovered by Joybubbles (A.K.A. Joe Engressia ) , who could whistle the 2600 MHz to tell the phone call to be a free call internationally. Later, Joe Engressia’s friend Captain Crunch (A.K.A. John Draper) earned his name from using a whistle prize from a captain crunch to get free phone calls. Many people used similar methods to get free calls then, though eventually the founders of Apple Inc. Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak tarted to make and sell “Blue Boxes” in October 1971 before the first Macintosh was made. Blue boxes were a device that would connect to the phone line and automatically play the proper tone to gain access to free calls. Phone Phreaking became a issue for AT&T, who nearly had a monopoly over phone lines at the time; Because of the monopoly phone phreaking became associated with political radicalism, resistance to capitalism, monopoly, and hacktivism. ( https://www.britannica.com/topic/phreaking )
Hacktivism (hacking activism)
Hacktivism is the act of civil disobedience to show unpleasant emotion toward a current political event or government, in most cases to push a political agenda. The term hacktivism was first used by the hacker group named Omega, who were communicating to Chinese hackers to combat rights abuse in China over email. While not created by them the term Hacktivism was highly attributed to Cult of the Dead Cow who claimed to be a neo communist hacker group. ( cult of the dead cow book )
Cult of the Dead Cow>
While never having more than twenty members at a time Cult of the Dead, jokingly got its name from a Texas slaughterhouse, cow cDc earned its place in history as one of the major hacking groups in the world. cDc was presented as if it was a form of performance art; they would use graphics and public events like conventions to get their agenda out. cDc was the first group to host a convention and invite law enforcement and government officials. HoHoCon, sponsored by DrunkFux ad hosted for five years annually, was a major success in finding new cDc members and reaching to targeted or new audiences that other black hat hacker cons have avoided. Along with the Cult of the dead cow’s popularity they made many hacker tools that are still used today such as Metasploit and back Orifice that hame made many ways to hack a lot more efficient. ( cult of the dead cow book )
L0pht
Brian Oblivion and Count Zero founded the l0pht in hopes to make an in person workspace for hackers. Brian Oblivion and Count zero used part of their wives’ hat making business. They would experiment on their own computers, things from the Flea at MIT, or from places Dumpsters after dumpster diving. L0pht would eventually become a Business itself as L0pht Heavy industries. L0pht released many security advisories and tools such as l0phtcrack and POCSAG.
L0pht senate testimony
The members of l0pht stood before the United States Congress on May 19, 1998 claiming that they could shut the entire internet down in just 30 minutes. The companies don’t care about their own security, its not the computers, the links between them or any of the devices it was the companies lack of caring for security. “if you are looking for computer security the internet is not the place to be,”. ( https://www.washingtonpost.com/sf/business/2015/06/22/net-of-insecurity-part-3/ ) History is hard to finish as there are still many problems in today’s cyber security.
